Reading the Major Arcana: A Beginner's Field Guide

by Madison · 12 min read · ☾ filed under spooky things

So you bought a tarot deck (probably impulsively, at 1am, like me) and now it's sitting on your shelf radiating mysterious energy and mild guilt. Good news: you don't need to be a thousand-year-old witch to start reading it. You just need a quiet hour and a willingness to be a little woo-woo about it.

This guide covers the Major Arcana — the 22 big, dramatic, capital-L Life cards. They're the headliners of the deck, and learning them is the fastest way to feel like you actually know what's going on.

What is the Major Arcana, anyway?

The Major Arcana is the deck's main storyline — a journey from the wide-eyed Fool (card 0) all the way to the World (card XXI). When one of these shows up in a reading, it's the universe tapping the table and saying pay attention, this one matters.

"The cards don't predict the future. They hand you a flashlight and point it at the part of yourself you've been avoiding." — something I wrote on a sticky note and now believe completely

What you'll need

  • a candle (vibes are non-negotiable)
  • a journal to scribble what each card stirs up
  • your deck — yes, the one still in shrink-wrap
  • one quiet hour with your phone face-down

"start with the first three cards — they teach you the whole grammar of the deck."

The first three arcana

Don't try to memorize all 22 at once. Learn these three deeply and the rest will start to make sense by relation.

  1. 0 — The Fool. Fresh starts, leaps of faith, that thrilling/terrifying feeling of stepping off the cliff with no plan and a backpack full of hope.
  2. I — The Magician. You already have every tool you need; this card is the nudge to actually use them and turn intention into something real.
  3. II — The High Priestess. Intuition, secrets, the quiet inner voice you keep talking over. She says: stop forcing it and listen.

A note on reversals

When a card shows up upside-down, don't panic. A reversal usually just turns the energy inward, or signals it's blocked or budding — not that doom is imminent. (You're allowed to ignore reversals entirely while learning. I did.)

🌿 Herb pairing Burn a little mugwort before a reading. It's the classic "open up your intuition" herb — earthy, dreamy, and it makes the whole ritual feel intentional. (Crack a window. Cosmoo hates the smell.)

That's it. Light the candle, shuffle until it feels right, pull three cards, and write down whatever your gut says before you Google a single meaning. Your intuition is the actual point. The little booklet is just training wheels. ♥
