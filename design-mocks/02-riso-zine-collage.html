Reading the Major Arcana: A Beginner's Field Guide

by Madison · 12 min read · filed under the witchcraft corner

The Major Arcana are the twenty-two big, loud, dramatic cards in a tarot deck — the ones with names like Death and The Tower that make people gasp at parties. They aren't here to predict your doom. Think of them as a comic strip about being a person: a journey from naive beginnings to hard-won wisdom, drawn in archetypes.

This is the field guide I wish I'd had when I bought my first deck off a folding table at a flea market. No gatekeeping, no thirty-dollar PDFs. Just the basics, scribbled in the margins.

Why The Major Arcana Matter

When a Major card shows up in a reading, it's the universe turning up the volume. Minor cards are the day-to-day weather; the Majors are the seasons. Learning them is less about memorizing keywords and more about recognizing yourself in the pictures.

"The cards don't tell the future. They tell you what you already half-know but keep refusing to look at."

What you'll need

  • one candle (any color, mine's always half-melted)
  • a journal you don't mind getting wax on
  • your deck — borrowed, gifted, or thrifted is fine
  • one quiet hour with the phone face-down
start with The Fool. always start with The Fool.

The First Three Cards

Don't try to learn all twenty-two at once — that way lies a closed deck and a dusty shelf. Start with these three. They open the whole story.

  1. The Fool — pure beginnings, the leap before you've checked for a net. Trust, foolishness, and the courage to start anyway.
  2. The Magician — you have every tool you need already on the table; this card is the nudge to actually use them.
  3. The High Priestess — the quiet inner voice, intuition, and the secrets you keep even from yourself.

Herb pairing: mugwort

Burn a little mugwort (or just keep a sprig by the deck) before a reading — it's the classic "open the third eye" herb. Mostly it smells nice and gives your hands something to do while you shuffle.

That's it for the field guide v1. I'll add the rest of the arcana as I get to them. Until then: light the candle, shuffle slow, and don't be afraid of the scary-looking cards. They're usually the kind ones.
