    Reading the Major Arcana: A Beginner's Field Guide

    by Madison · 12 min read · filed under candle-wax & intuition

    There is a particular hush that settles over a room when you lay the first card face-down on the table. The Major Arcana — twenty-two cards numbered zero through twenty-one — are the spine of any deck, the grand archetypes that narrate a life from foolish beginning to whole completion. This field guide is the one I wish someone had handed me on the night I first shuffled, candle guttering, convinced I was doing everything wrong.

    Before You Shuffle

    Apothecary

    Herb pairing: mugwort

    Burned or brewed weak, mugwort is the traditional companion for divination — said to sharpen dream-sight. A pinch under the pillow, a sprig by the deck.

    You do not need anything expensive to begin. You need quiet, a little ceremony, and a willingness to sit with the images long enough that they start speaking back. Pick a deck whose art you actually like looking at; you will be staring at it a lot.

    What you'll need

    • A candle, lit and given a moment to settle
    • A journal to record what you pulled
    • Your deck — borrowed, gifted, or thrifted
    • One quiet hour with the door closed
    The cards do not predict the future. They hold up a mirror and ask whether you've been honest with yourself.

    The First Three Cards

    Every journey through the Arcana starts the same way — with naïveté, with will, and with secret knowledge. Learn these three and the rest of the deck unfolds like a familiar story.

    1. 0The Fool. Pure potential and the leap before looking; a beginning so open it borders on reckless faith.
    2. IThe Magician. Will made manifest — all four elements on the table and the focus to bend them to a purpose.
    3. IIThe High Priestess. The veil, the moon, the things known but unspoken; intuition over intellect.
    Read slowly. The deck rewards the patient and confuses the hurried.

    A note on reversals

    When a card lands upside-down, resist the urge to call it "bad." A reversal is a dimmer switch, not an off switch — the same energy, blocked, internalized, or asking for attention. Note it, sit with it, and let the journal do the remembering so your intuition can do the reading.
