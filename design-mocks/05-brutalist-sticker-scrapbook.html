Reading the Major Arcana: A Beginner's Field Guide

by Madison · 12 min read

So you bought a tarot deck on impulse (relatable) and now twenty-two strange, beautiful cards are staring at you. Don't panic. The Major Arcana isn't a test — it's a story you already know, told in pictures. This is the field guide I wish someone had handed me when I started.

The Majors are the "big" cards: the milestones, the archetypes, the plot beats of a life. When one shows up in a reading, the universe is basically tapping you on the shoulder. We're going to walk through how to actually read them instead of just memorizing meanings off the little booklet (we've all been there).

First, ditch the fear

The most-feared cards — Death, The Tower, The Devil — are almost never literal. Death means transformation. The Tower means a much-needed shake-up. The cards are honest, not cruel. Approach them like a friend who tells you the truth because they love you.

"The cards don't predict the future. They describe the present so clearly that the future becomes obvious."

How a reading actually feels

Less "fortune teller in a tent," more "journaling with a really wise stranger." You pull a card, you sit with the image, you notice what your gut says before you look anything up. That gut reaction is the reading. Everything else is decoration.

✶ What you'll need

  • A candle — for ambiance and a little ceremony
  • A journal — write the card + your first gut reaction
  • Your deck — whichever one called to you in the shop
  • A quiet hour — phone face-down, notifications off

Read the picture first. The book second. Always.

The first three cards

If you only learn three to start, learn these. They're the opening act of the whole deck, and once you feel them, the rest start clicking into place.

  1. 0The Fool — the leap of faith. Beginnings, innocence, saying yes before you're ready. You at the start of literally anything.
  2. IThe Magician — you already have every tool you need. Focus, willpower, turning ideas into reality. Stop waiting.
  3. IIThe High Priestess — the quiet knowing. Intuition, secrets, the answer you can feel but can't yet explain.
Herb pairing: mugwort A little dried mugwort near your reading space is the classic intuition booster. Tuck a sprig in your journal or burn a tiny bit. Smells like a witchy attic. Perfect.

That's it. That's the start. Pull one card tomorrow morning, write your gut reaction, and don't look it up until the evening. Do that for a week and you'll be reading the Majors like you've known them your whole life — because, honestly, you kind of have.
