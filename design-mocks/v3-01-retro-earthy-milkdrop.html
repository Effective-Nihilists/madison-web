Reading the Major Arcana: A Beginner's Field Guide

by Madison · 12 min read

The Major Arcana are the loud, dramatic cards of the tarot — the soap opera, the hero's journey, the part where the music swells. If the Minor Arcana are everyday weather, the Majors are the seasons. Here's how to start reading them without feeling like you need a PhD in vibes.

Why start with the Majors?

There are only 22 of them, each a self-contained little story. Learn these and you've got the spine of every spread you'll ever pull. Don't memorize keyword lists — memorize the feeling.

"The cards don't predict the future. They give you a flashlight to look at the present." — something I wrote on a napkin once

What you'll need

  • a candle (any color, but amber feels right)
  • a journal you don't mind scribbling in
  • your deck — borrowed decks count
  • one quiet hour with the phone face-down
"learn the feeling, not the keyword list."

Three to know first

  1. 0The Fool — a leap of faith with nothing but a backpack and good intentions.
  2. IThe Magician — you already have every tool you need; now use them.
  3. IIThe High Priestess — the answer is quiet, internal, and you already half-know it.
Herb pairing: mugwort — burn a little (safely!) before a reading to nudge the dreaming mind awake.
