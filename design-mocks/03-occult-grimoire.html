A Margin Note, Scrawled by Candlelight

today, 2:14am

Can't sleep again. The moon is doing that thing where it looks like a thumbnail of light pressed into black wax, and I keep thinking how strange it is that we just agreed the stars meant something — agreed it so hard it became true enough to read a life by. I reorganized my whole tarot shelf at midnight. I rewatched the same comfort movie twice. Tomorrow I'll pretend I'm a functional person. Tonight I'm just a small animal made of static, awake in a little corner of the internet.

Reading the Major Arcana: A Beginner's Field Guide

by Madison · 12 min read

❖❖ ❖❖

There is a particular hush that settles over a room the first time you lay the Major Arcana out in a line. Twenty-two cards, twenty-two doorways — and somewhere in that procession, a story that is, suspiciously, always about you. This guide is the one I wish someone had pressed into my hands the night I bought my first deck, terrified I would do it wrong. Spoiler: you cannot do it wrong. You can only do it honestly.

The Major Arcana is the spine of the tarot — the big, archetypal beats. Where the Minor Arcana frets over the daily weather of a life, the Majors speak in seasons. Learn to read them and you have learned to read the shape of a chapter, not just a sentence.

☽

Before You Begin

Treat this less like studying and more like making a friend. Sit with the cards. Let them be strange. The meanings below are scaffolding, not scripture — once you know the rules, you are allowed, even encouraged, to feel your way past them.

What you'll need

  • A candle — beeswax if you have it, for the honeyed light
  • A journal, kept only for readings
  • Your deck (any deck; the one that called to you is the right one)
  • One quiet hour with the phone face-down in another room
"The cards do not predict the future. They describe the present so clearly that the future stops being a surprise."
☿

How to Actually Read a Card

Pull one card. Don't reach for the booklet yet. Look at it the way you'd look at a stranger across a train carriage — what's the posture, the colour, the weather of the thing? Your first impression is data. The traditional meaning is a second opinion, not the verdict.

Once the image has spoken, layer the keywords on top. The art tells you how it feels; the tradition tells you what it tends to mean. Held together, they become a sentence only you could have written.

❝ Every spread is a self-portrait drawn in a borrowed hand.
✦

The First Three Arcana

We begin, as the deck does, at zero. Learn these three intimately and the rest of the procession will start to feel like family arriving for a long dinner.

  1. 0The Fool.  The held breath before the leap — beginnings, innocence, and a faith that borders on recklessness. He carries almost nothing, and that is the point.
  2. IThe Magician.  All four elements on the table and the will to combine them — manifestation, focus, the moment you realise you already have the tools.
  3. IIThe High Priestess.  The veiled keeper of the unsaid — intuition, secrets, and the deep water you're not yet meant to name. She rewards patience over force.
⚗  Apothecary  ⚗
Herb Pairing — Mugwort

Burn a pinch before a reading to loosen the dreaming mind, or tuck it beneath your pillow for prophetic sleep. Steep it weakly; it is bitter and it does not need much to be heard.

That's the threshold. From here it's just practice and patience — and the quiet thrill of noticing, months from now, that the cards have started telling you things you already knew but hadn't admitted. Welcome to the work. So mote it be.
